Why this order? Start with what cybercrime is before jumping to laws — understanding the crime makes the Act make sense. Phishing & Identity Theft come before the IT Act because they are the most concrete examples the law addresses. Attack tools (DoS, SQL) are studied after you know the crime types, so each technique has context. Mobile security is a full Unit 2 with 15-mark potential — don't skip. The IT Act is studied after the crimes so sections feel logical, not arbitrary. Digital Signatures come last among Must Do topics — they need the cryptography mindset. Cyber Ethics wraps it all up as the newest high-risk topic for 2026.
