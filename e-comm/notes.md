# **UNIT 1 - Overview of E-Commerce**

_Priority: MUST DO | PYQ Frequency: ★★★★★ every year_

## **1.1 What is E-Commerce?**

**Definition:** E-Commerce (Electronic Commerce) is the buying, selling, marketing, and servicing of goods and services over computer networks (primarily the Internet), where transactions involve the transfer of ownership or rights to use goods/services.

- It covers the entire trading process - browsing, ordering, payment, and delivery - conducted electronically.
- E-Commerce is a SUBSET of E-Business; E-Business also includes internal processes like HR, manufacturing, and supply chain that don't directly involve a transaction.
- *Sale or purchase of items without physically visiting a shop* is a one-line definition style frequently asked in Group A. _(PYQ 2023 Grp-A)_

### **1.1.1 E-Business vs E-Commerce** ★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: "Distinguish between e-business and e-commerce" appeared directly in 2024 Group C Q10(a). Memorise this table._

| **Dimension** | **E-Commerce** | **E-Business** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Scope | Narrower - focused on buying/selling transactions online | Broader - covers ALL business processes conducted using digital technology |
| Includes | Online transactions, payments, order processing | E-commerce + internal operations: HR, supply chain, CRM, ERP, manufacturing |
| Goal | Facilitate commercial transactions | Improve overall business efficiency using IT |
| Examples | Online shopping on Amazon, online ticket booking | Amazon's entire digital operation including warehouse automation, supplier portals |
| Relationship | E-Commerce is a PART of E-Business | E-Business is the SUPERSET |
| Analogy | The cash register (transaction point) | The entire store + back office (everything) |

## **1.2 Advantages of E-Commerce** ★★★★★

Group by perspective - this is how exams frame it ("discuss benefits to organisation/customer/society"):

**To the Organisation:**
- Global market reach without geographical limitation
- Lower operational costs (no physical storefront, reduced staff)
- Faster transaction processing and order fulfilment
- Better customer relationship management through data collection
- 24×7 business availability

**To the Customer:**
- Convenience - shop anytime, anywhere
- Wide product comparison and choice
- Often lower prices due to reduced overhead
- Home delivery and doorstep service

**To Society:**
- Enables work-from-home/telecommuting, reducing traffic congestion
- Allows people in remote areas to access goods/services
- Reduces the price of many goods through increased competition

_📝 EXAM TIP: "Write down the benefits of electronic web commerce" (2023 Grp-C Q7a) and "Discuss how E-Commerce is helpful to business success" (2023, 2024 - repeated) are near-guaranteed. Prepare a 5-mark and 10-mark version of this answer._

## **1.3 Disadvantages of E-Commerce** ★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: "Main disadvantages of E-Commerce" - direct 5-mark question (2024 Grp-B Q3)._

- **Security concerns** - risk of fraud, hacking, data breaches, payment theft
- **Lack of personal touch** - no physical inspection of goods before purchase
- **Technology dependency** - requires internet access and digital literacy; excludes those without access (digital divide)
- **Trust issues** - customers may distrust unfamiliar sellers
- **High initial setup cost** - building secure infrastructure, payment gateways
- **Legal & jurisdictional issues** - laws differ across countries, hard to enforce
- **Delivery delays & logistics challenges** - especially in remote areas
- **Product return/refund complications**

## **1.4 Tangible vs Intangible Benefits of E-Business** ★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: Direct question 2024 Grp-C Q10(c) - "Write about Tangible and Intangible benefits of e-business."_

| **Type** | **Meaning** | **Examples** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Tangible Benefits | Measurable, quantifiable benefits directly impacting cost/revenue | Reduced inventory cost, lower marketing cost, increased sales volume, reduced order-processing time |
| Intangible Benefits | Non-measurable benefits that improve quality/perception over time | Improved brand image, better customer satisfaction, improved customer service, competitive advantage |

## **1.5 Threats of E-Commerce** ★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: "Discuss the threats of E-Commerce" (2024 Grp-B Q4) - direct 5-mark question._

- **Phishing & social engineering** - tricking users into revealing credentials
- **Malware/viruses/ransomware** attacking servers or customer devices
- **Denial of Service (DoS/DDoS) attacks** - taking down e-commerce websites
- **Credit card/payment fraud** - stolen card details used for unauthorised purchases
- **SQL Injection & website hacking** - stealing customer databases
- **Man-in-the-Middle attacks** - intercepting data during transmission
- **Identity theft** - impersonation using stolen personal data
- **Insider threats** - employees misusing access to customer/financial data

## **1.6 Managerial Perspective on E-Commerce**

From a managerial standpoint, E-Commerce adoption requires balancing:

- **Strategic alignment** - does the e-commerce initiative support overall business goals?
- **Investment decisions** - cost of technology infrastructure vs expected ROI
- **Risk management** - legal, security, and operational risks
- **Change management** - training staff, restructuring processes for digital operations
- **Customer relationship strategy** - how digital channels integrate with traditional customer service

Managers must evaluate e-commerce not just as a sales channel but as a transformation of the entire value chain - marketing, finance, supply chain, and customer service.

## **1.7 Rules & Regulations for Controlling E-Commerce; Cyber Laws** ★★★

**Why regulation is needed:** E-commerce transactions cross borders, involve sensitive financial data, and lack the physical safeguards of traditional commerce - making legal frameworks essential for trust and dispute resolution.

Key regulatory areas in India:
- **Information Technology Act, 2000 (IT Act)** - India's primary cyber law governing electronic transactions, digital signatures, and cybercrime
- **Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules, 2020** - mandates disclosure of seller details, grievance redressal, prohibits unfair trade practices on e-commerce platforms
- **Payment and Settlement Systems Act, 2007** - regulates electronic payment systems
- **RBI Guidelines** - governs digital payment gateways, KYC norms for online transactions
- **Data Protection laws** - safeguard customer personal/financial information (Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023)

Core legal concerns addressed: validity of electronic contracts/digital signatures, jurisdiction in cross-border disputes, consumer rights and grievance redressal, taxation of online transactions (GST), and intellectual property protection online.

# **UNIT 2 - Technologies & Networking for E-Commerce**

_Priority: MUST DO | PYQ Frequency: ★★★★ every year_

## **2.1 Relationship Between E-Commerce & Networking**

E-Commerce is fundamentally dependent on networking infrastructure - without interconnected computer networks (the Internet being the largest), no electronic transaction can take place.

- The **Internet** acts as the global backbone connecting buyers, sellers, and payment gateways
- **Network protocols** (TCP/IP, HTTP/HTTPS) enable reliable data transfer for transactions
- **Network security** (firewalls, SSL/TLS) protects the data flowing through these networks during commerce
- Without networking: no real-time price updates, no instant order confirmation, no online payment processing

## **2.2 Internet, Intranet & Extranet** ★★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: "Distinguish between Internet and Intranet" / "Compare and contrast Extranet and Intranet" has appeared in BOTH 2023 and 2024 papers. This is a near-certain Group B/C question._

| **Dimension** | **Internet** | **Intranet** | **Extranet** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Definition | A global, public network of interconnected networks accessible to anyone | A private network restricted to within an organisation, using internet technologies | A controlled private network that extends to selected outside parties (suppliers, partners) |
| Access | Open to the public worldwide | Restricted to employees/internal users only | Restricted to authorised external users (partners, vendors, customers) via login |
| Ownership | No single owner - decentralised | Owned and controlled by one organisation | Shared control - organisation + external partners |
| Security | Lower - open access, higher risk | High - protected by firewall, internal only | Medium-High - firewall + authentication for external access |
| Purpose | Global communication, public information, e-commerce | Internal communication, document sharing, internal apps | B2B collaboration, supply chain coordination, partner data sharing |
| Example Use | Browsing websites, online shopping, email | Company's internal HR portal, internal wiki | Supplier portal for placing/tracking orders with a manufacturer |
| Analogy | A public highway open to all | A private road inside a gated company campus | A private road that lets selected outside vehicles enter with a pass |

_Memory trick: **"Internet is for everyone, Intranet is for IN-house, Extranet EXTends to outsiders."**_

## **2.3 Different Types of Networking Commerce**

Networking-based commerce models can be classified by the scope of network access:

- **Internet-based commerce** - open, public commerce (most B2C e-commerce: Amazon, Flipkart)
- **Intranet-based commerce** - internal organisational transactions (internal procurement systems)
- **Extranet-based commerce** - B2B commerce between trusted partners (supplier-manufacturer ordering systems, EDI-based trade)
- **VAN-based commerce (Value Added Network)** - private, dedicated networks used historically for secure EDI transactions between large trading partners

## **2.4 EDI Systems - Introductory Overview**

**EDI (Electronic Data Interchange):** The direct computer-to-computer transfer of transaction information contained in standard, structured business documents (purchase orders, invoices, shipping notices) - without human intervention or paper. _(Definition style asked directly: "What is the name for direct computer-to-computer transfer..." PYQ 2025 Grp-A)_

> Full EDI architecture, protocols, and benefits are covered in **Unit 10** below - this section is the introductory placement per syllabus order.

## **2.5 Wireless Application Protocol (WAP)** ★★★

**Definition:** WAP is a technical standard/protocol for accessing information over a mobile wireless network. It enables mobile devices with limited processing power, memory, and small screens to access internet-based content and services in a format suitable for the device.

- Designed to work with limited bandwidth and small displays of handheld devices
- Uses **WML (Wireless Markup Language)** instead of HTML to render content suitable for small screens
- WAP Gateway acts as a translator between the wireless network and the internet

**3 Limitations of WAP for mobile computing:**
1. Limited display size restricts the amount/format of content shown
2. Slower data transfer speeds compared to fixed broadband connections
3. Limited security compared to full internet protocols (early versions had the "WAP Gap" security issue at the gateway)

## **2.6 Hand-Held Devices, Mobility & Commerce**

Hand-held devices (smartphones, tablets, PDAs) enable **M-Commerce (Mobile Commerce)** - the subset of e-commerce conducted via wireless handheld devices.

**Two applications of M-Commerce** _(commonly asked: "Give two applications of M-Commerce" PYQ 2024 Grp-A)_:
- Mobile banking and mobile payment apps (UPI, mobile wallets)
- Location-based services - food delivery, ride-hailing apps using GPS

Other examples: mobile ticket booking, in-app shopping, mobile stock trading.

## **2.7 Mobile Computing**

**Mobile Computing:** A technology paradigm that allows transmission of data, voice, and video via a computer or any other wireless-enabled device without being connected to a fixed physical link - enabling users to access and process information from anywhere.

Key components: mobile hardware (smartphones, laptops), mobile software (mobile OS, apps), and mobile communication (cellular networks, Wi-Fi, Bluetooth).

## **2.8 Wireless Web**

The **Wireless Web** refers to internet content and services accessed via wireless networks (3G/4G/5G, Wi-Fi) rather than wired connections - the foundation that makes M-Commerce possible. It involves adapting web content (often using responsive design or WAP/WML in early systems) for varying device capabilities and intermittent connectivity.

## **2.9 Web Security - Introductory Overview**

Basic web security concerns in e-commerce networking include:

- **SSL/TLS encryption** - secures data in transit between browser and server
- **Firewalls** - filter unauthorised network traffic (detailed in Unit 11)
- **Authentication mechanisms** - verifying user identity before granting access

> Detailed security standards, cryptography, and digital certificates are covered comprehensively in **Unit 11**.

## **2.10 Infrastructure Requirements for E-Commerce**

For a functioning e-commerce system, the following infrastructure is required:

- **Hardware** - servers, networking equipment, client devices
- **Software** - web server software, database management systems, e-commerce platform/CMS
- **Network connectivity** - reliable, high-bandwidth internet connection
- **Payment gateway integration** - to process online transactions securely
- **Security infrastructure** - firewalls, SSL certificates, encryption tools
- **Logistics & fulfilment systems** - integration with warehousing and delivery/courier systems
- **Customer support systems** - chatbots, helpdesk, call centers

# **UNIT 3 - Business Models of E-Commerce**

_Priority: MUST DO | PYQ Frequency: ★★★★★ every year_

## **3.1 Models Based on Transaction Type**

E-Commerce business models can be classified based on the **type of transaction** (the nature of activity):

- **Brokerage Model** - bringing buyers and sellers together and charging a fee/commission per transaction (e.g., eBay, real estate portals)
- **Advertising Model** - generating revenue by displaying ads to a large user base (e.g., Google, social media)
- **Subscription Model** - charging users a recurring fee for access to content/services (e.g., Netflix, online journals)
- **Merchant Model** - traditional wholesalers/retailers selling goods directly online (e.g., Amazon's own inventory sales)

## **3.2 Models Based on Transaction Party** ★★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: "What are the 4 C's of Digital Marketing" is a trap - do NOT confuse with the 4 C's of E-Commerce (Unit 5). This section's "4 types" (B2B, B2C, C2B, C2C) is the core syllabus content and appears EVERY year._

This is the **most important classification** in the syllabus - based on WHO is transacting with WHOM.

| **Model** | **Full Form** | **Definition** | **Example** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| B2B | Business-to-Business | Transactions between two businesses - e.g., manufacturer selling to wholesaler, or supplier providing raw materials to a factory | IndiaMART, Alibaba, a steel manufacturer selling to a car company |
| B2C | Business-to-Consumer | Transactions between a business and an individual end consumer | Amazon, Flipkart, Myntra |
| C2B | Consumer-to-Business | Individual consumers offer products/services to businesses, or set their own price for a business to accept | Freelancing platforms (Upwork), influencer marketing offers, "name your price" travel sites |
| C2C | Consumer-to-Consumer | Transactions directly between individual consumers, typically facilitated by a third-party platform | OLX, eBay (peer listings), Facebook Marketplace - _OLX given as direct example in PYQ 2025 Grp-A_ |

### **3.2.1 B2B vs B2C - Detailed Comparison** ★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: "Compared to B2C e-commerce, B2B e-commerce is ___" (fill-in, PYQ 2023) - the expected answer relates to LARGER transaction volume/value but FEWER transactions. "Discuss the different E-Commerce business models based on transaction type" (2024 Grp-C, 8 marks) wants this full table._

| **Dimension** | **B2B** | **B2C** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Parties involved | Business to Business | Business to individual Consumer |
| Transaction volume | Fewer transactions but much HIGHER value per transaction | Many transactions but LOWER value per transaction |
| Decision-making | Complex, often involves multiple stakeholders/approval chain | Simple, usually a single individual's decision |
| Sales cycle | Longer - negotiation, contracts, bulk pricing | Shorter - often instant purchase |
| Relationship | Long-term, relationship-based (repeat ordering) | Often one-off or short-term per purchase |
| Key technologies | EDI, extranets, supply chain integration systems | Web storefronts, shopping carts, mobile apps |
| Example | A car manufacturer ordering components from a parts supplier | A customer buying a phone from Flipkart |

**Key technologies of B2B E-Commerce** _(PYQ 2024 Grp-B)_: EDI (Electronic Data Interchange) for automated document exchange, Extranets for secure partner access, ERP system integration for order-to-cash automation, and digital catalogues/marketplaces for procurement.

**Architectural model of B2B E-Commerce:** Typically involves a buyer's procurement system connected via EDI/Extranet to the seller's order management system, with a payment/settlement layer and logistics tracking integrated through APIs or VANs (Value Added Networks).

## **3.3 E-Governance** ★★★

**Definition:** E-Governance refers to the use of Information and Communication Technology (ICT) by government agencies to deliver public services, exchange information, and conduct transactions with citizens, businesses, and other government entities efficiently and transparently.

**Challenges of E-Governance in India** _(PYQ 2024 Grp-C Q7c)_:
- **Digital divide** - unequal access to internet/devices across rural and urban India
- **Digital literacy** - many citizens lack the skills to use e-governance platforms
- **Infrastructure gaps** - inconsistent internet connectivity, especially in remote regions
- **Cybersecurity & privacy concerns** - protecting citizen data from breaches
- **Language barriers** - most portals are limited to English/Hindi, excluding regional language speakers
- **Interoperability** - different government departments use disparate, non-integrated systems
- **Resistance to change** - bureaucratic and procedural resistance to digital transformation

# **UNIT 4 - E-Strategy**

_Priority: MEDIUM (less syllabus weight, but answer-ready) | PYQ Frequency: ★★ low-direct but blends into Group C_

## **4.1 Overview of E-Strategy**

**E-Strategy:** The process of formulating a plan to use electronic means (internet, digital technologies) to achieve an organisation's overall business objectives - aligning digital initiatives with corporate strategy.

E-strategy answers: *which digital channels to invest in, how to integrate online and offline operations, and how to gain competitive advantage through technology.*

## **4.2 Strategic Methods for Developing E-Commerce**

Common strategic approaches organisations use when developing their e-commerce presence:

- **Market analysis & SWOT analysis** - assessing Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats before digital investment
- **Competitive positioning** - analysing competitors' online presence to differentiate
- **Phased implementation** - starting with a basic web presence, then scaling to full transactional capability
- **Partnership & alliance strategy** - tying up with payment gateways, logistics providers, technology vendors
- **Customer-centric design** - building the e-commerce strategy around customer journey and experience
- **Technology infrastructure planning** - choosing scalable platforms, cloud hosting, security architecture
- **Performance measurement** - using KPIs (conversion rate, cart abandonment, customer acquisition cost) to refine strategy over time

_📝 EXAM TIP: If asked to design an e-strategy for a hypothetical business (common Group C scenario question), structure your answer as: Goal → SWOT → Target Market → Technology Choice → Marketing Plan → Metrics._

# **UNIT 5 - The Four C's of E-Commerce**

_Priority: MUST DO | PYQ Frequency: ★★★★★ every year_

_Memory trick: **"Convergence Connects, Collaboration Creates, Content Controls, Call centers Care"** — Convergence, Collaborative Computing, Content Management, Call Center._

## **5.1 Convergence** ★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: "Define Convergence, Collaborative Computing, Content Management & Call Center" appeared as a direct 10-mark question (PYQ 2023 Grp-C Q9a). Prepare crisp 2-3 line definitions PLUS one example for each._

**Definition:** Convergence refers to the integration/merging of previously distinct technologies - computing, telecommunications, and media/broadcasting - into a single unified platform or device, enabling seamless delivery of voice, data, and video.

**Types of Convergence (Technological Advances):**
- **Device convergence** - a single device performing multiple functions (smartphone = phone + camera + computer)
- **Network convergence** - voice, data, and video traveling over the same IP network (VoIP)
- **Media convergence** - traditional media (TV, print) merging with digital platforms (streaming, online news)

**Implications of Convergence:**
- Reduces cost of maintaining separate infrastructure for voice/data/video
- Enables new business models (e.g., triple-play services: internet + phone + TV from one provider)
- Increases complexity of regulation (telecom vs broadcasting laws overlapping)

**Convergence & Electronic Commerce:** Convergence directly enables e-commerce by allowing customers to browse, communicate, and transact using a single device/network - e.g., making a purchase via a mobile app that also handles calls, messaging, and video support chat, all over one data connection.

## **5.2 Collaborative Computing** ★★★

**Definition:** Collaborative Computing refers to technology systems that enable multiple individuals or teams (often geographically dispersed) to work together on shared tasks, documents, or designs in real time or asynchronously, using networked computing resources.

- **Collaborative product development** - teams across locations jointly designing a product using shared digital tools
- **Contract as per CAD (Computer-Aided Design)** - engineering/design contracts increasingly specify deliverables in CAD file formats so distributed teams can collaborate on the same design data
- **Simultaneous Collaboration** - multiple users editing/viewing the same document or model at the same time (e.g., shared CAD sessions, simultaneous engineering)
- **Security in Collaborative Computing** - since multiple external parties access shared systems, strong access control, encryption, and version control are essential to prevent unauthorised changes or data leaks

## **5.3 Content Management** ★★★★

**Definition of Content:** In e-commerce, "content" refers to all digital information presented to users - text, images, video, product descriptions, reviews - that informs, persuades, or engages customers.

**Authoring Tools & Content Management:** Authoring tools are software applications used to create and edit digital content (CMS platforms like WordPress, Adobe tools). A **Content Management System (CMS)** organises, stores, and publishes this content systematically, allowing non-technical staff to update website content without coding.

**Content - related concepts:**
- **Content partnership** - agreements with other organisations to share or syndicate content (e.g., a retailer partnering with a magazine for product reviews)
- **Content repositories** - centralised storage systems where digital content/assets are stored and managed
- **Content convergence** - same content being repurposed across multiple channels (web, mobile app, social media)
- **Content providers** - third parties that supply content (news feeds, stock images, reviews) to an e-commerce site

**Web Traffic & Traffic Management:** Refers to monitoring and analysing the volume of visitors to a website and managing server/network resources to handle that load efficiently (load balancing, CDN usage) while also using traffic data for marketing insights.

**Content Marketing:** A strategic marketing approach focused on creating and distributing valuable, relevant content (blogs, videos, guides) to attract and retain a target audience, ultimately driving profitable customer action - rather than directly advertising products.

## **5.4 Call Center** ★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: "What is call center? Discuss about the call center equipment" + "modes of operation" + "CPE" appeared TOGETHER as a multi-part 15-mark question (PYQ 2025 Grp-C Q10). This is a high-yield combined topic._

**Definition:** A Call Center is a centralised office/department within an organisation that handles a large volume of telephone (and increasingly digital/chat) interactions with customers - for support, sales, or service purposes.

**Need for Call Centers:** Provide a single point of contact for customer queries, enable 24×7 customer support, improve customer satisfaction through quick issue resolution, and gather customer feedback/data centrally.

**Tasks Handled by Call Centers:**
- Inbound customer support and complaint resolution
- Outbound telemarketing and sales calls
- Order processing and tracking queries
- Technical support / helpdesk
- Appointment scheduling and reminder calls

**Modes of Operation of Call Center:**
- **Inbound mode** - agents receive calls from customers (support, queries)
- **Outbound mode** - agents make calls to customers (sales, surveys, collections)
- **Blended mode** - combination of inbound and outbound handled by the same team
- **Virtual call center** - agents work remotely, connected via VoIP/cloud systems rather than a single physical location

**Call Center Equipment:**
- Automatic Call Distributor (ACD) - routes incoming calls to available agents
- Interactive Voice Response (IVR) system - automated menu-based call handling
- Headsets and telephony hardware for agents
- Computer Telephony Integration (CTI) systems linking phone systems with customer databases
- Customer Relationship Management (CRM) software for tracking interactions

**Strengths of Call Centers:** Centralised, consistent customer service; cost-efficient compared to physical branches; scalable; provides valuable customer interaction data.

**Weaknesses of Call Centers:** High employee turnover/burnout; can feel impersonal to customers; long wait times during high volume; language/communication barriers in outsourced centers.

### **5.4.1 Customer Premises Equipment (CPE)** ★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: "Explain the meaning of CPE. Give examples" was a direct 5-mark question (PYQ 2024 Grp-B Q6). Also asked combined with Denial of Service definition (PYQ 2025)._

**Definition:** Customer-Premises Equipment (CPE) refers to telecommunications and networking hardware located at the customer's (subscriber's) physical location, rather than at the service provider's premises, used to originate, route, or terminate communication.

**Examples of CPE:** Telephones, modems, routers, set-top boxes, fax machines, VoIP adapters, and customer-side networking equipment used to connect to a service provider's network.

# **UNIT 7 - Supply Chain Management (SCM)**

_Priority: STRONG | PYQ Frequency: ★★★★ every year_

## **7.1 What is Supply Chain Management?**

_📝 EXAM TIP: "What do you think is Supply Chain Management?" + "Why is SCM vital for e-commerce?" appear as paired questions across multiple years (2023, 2024). Always answer "why vital" with: cost reduction, faster delivery, real-time visibility, customer satisfaction._

**Definition:** Supply Chain Management is the coordinated management of the flow of goods, information, and finances as they move from supplier to manufacturer to wholesaler to retailer to the final consumer - encompassing planning, sourcing, production, and delivery.

**Supply chain of e-commerce** refers specifically to how an online business manages procurement, inventory, warehousing, and last-mile delivery to fulfil online orders efficiently.

**Why SCM is vital for E-Commerce:**
- Ensures products are available when customers order (avoiding stockouts)
- Reduces operational costs through optimised inventory and logistics
- Enables faster delivery, a key competitive differentiator in e-commerce
- Provides real-time order tracking and transparency to customers
- Helps manage returns/reverse logistics efficiently

## **7.2 E-Logistics**

**E-Logistics:** The use of internet-based technologies to manage the flow of goods, information, and services involved in the supply chain - including order processing, inventory management, warehousing, and transportation - all coordinated electronically for speed and visibility.

## **7.3 Supply Chain Portal**

**Supply Chain Portal:** A web-based platform that provides a single point of access for all supply chain partners (suppliers, manufacturers, distributors, retailers) to view order status, inventory levels, shipment tracking, and collaborate on planning - improving transparency across the entire chain.

**How supply chain portals help E-Commerce manage supply chain** _(PYQ 2023 Grp-C Q11d)_: by giving all stakeholders real-time visibility into stock levels and order status, enabling faster decision-making, reducing communication delays via email/phone, and allowing automated alerts for low stock or delayed shipments.

## **7.4 Supply Chain Planning Tools (SCP) vs Supply Chain Execution (SCE)** ★★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: This comparison is the single highest-yield topic in Unit 7 - the syllabus explicitly separates SCP and SCE, and exams test the distinction directly._

| **Dimension** | **Supply Chain Planning (SCP)** | **Supply Chain Execution (SCE)** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Focus | Forecasting, planning, and decision-making BEFORE operations happen | Carrying OUT the actual physical operations of the supply chain |
| Time orientation | Forward-looking - demand forecasting, "what should happen" | Real-time/present - "what is happening now" |
| Activities | Demand planning, inventory optimisation, production scheduling, network design | Order fulfilment, warehouse management, transportation management, shipping |
| Tools/Systems | Demand forecasting software, planning algorithms, simulation models | Warehouse Management Systems (WMS), Transportation Management Systems (TMS) |
| Goal | Optimise resources and reduce future uncertainty | Ensure accurate, timely, and efficient physical execution of plans |
| Example | Predicting next quarter's demand for a product and planning stock accordingly | Picking, packing, and shipping the actual order from the warehouse |

**SCE Framework:** A structured set of systems and processes for executing supply chain operations - typically comprising Warehouse Management (storage and picking), Transportation Management (routing and carrier selection), Order Management (tracking order lifecycle), and Labor Management (workforce allocation) - all integrated to ensure plans from SCP are carried out efficiently.

## **7.5 Internet's Effect on Supply Chain Power**

The Internet has shifted the balance of power within supply chains:

- **Increased buyer power** - customers can easily compare prices/products across multiple sellers, reducing supplier pricing power
- **Disintermediation** - the internet allows manufacturers to sell directly to consumers, bypassing traditional middlemen (wholesalers, retailers)
- **Reintermediation** - new types of online intermediaries emerge (e-marketplaces, aggregators) that consolidate supply chain functions in new ways
- **Greater transparency** - real-time data sharing reduces information asymmetry between supply chain partners
- **Faster response to demand changes** - internet-enabled forecasting allows supply chains to react more dynamically to market shifts

# **UNIT 8 - E-Payment Mechanism**

_Priority: STRONG | PYQ Frequency: ★★★★★ every year_

## **8.1 Overview of E-Payment Systems**

_📝 EXAM TIP: "Explain any 5 types of Electronic Payment Systems" is a guaranteed 10-mark Group C question (PYQ 2023 Grp-C Q8a). Be ready to explain at least 5 of the types below with one line each on how they work._

**Definition:** An Electronic Payment System (E-Payment) is any system that facilitates the transfer of money electronically over the internet for goods/services purchased online, eliminating the need for cash or physical cheques.

## **8.2 Payment Through Card System** ★★★★

Card-based payment is the most common e-payment method:

- **Credit Card** - customer borrows funds up to a limit from the issuing bank; payment is settled later (bill cycle)
- **Debit Card** - amount is immediately deducted from the customer's bank account at the time of purchase. _(Direct PYQ 2025 Grp-A: "name of the card...amount will be immediately debited from buyer's account" = Debit Card)_
- **Stored-Value Card** _(PYQ 2024 Grp-A)_ - a card pre-loaded with a fixed monetary value (e.g., gift cards, prepaid transit cards); value decreases with each use, no link to a bank account
- **Smart Card** - embedded microchip card capable of storing and processing data securely (used for secure authentication beyond just payment)

**SET (Secure Electronic Transaction) Protocol** _(PYQ 2025 Grp-A: "SET protocol is used for ___")_: A protocol jointly developed by Visa and Mastercard to secure card payments over open networks - ensures confidentiality of payment info, integrity of transmitted data, and authentication of both cardholder and merchant.

## **8.3 E-Cheque (Electronic Cheque)** ★★★

**Definition:** An E-Cheque is the electronic equivalent of a paper cheque - a digital document that instructs a bank to transfer funds from the payer's account to the payee's account, transmitted securely over a network, often using digital signatures for authentication.

- Functions like a paper cheque but is signed and transmitted digitally
- Cleared through electronic clearing systems rather than physical bank processing
- Provides a digital audit trail, reducing processing time compared to paper cheques

## **8.4 E-Cash (Electronic/Digital Cash)** ★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: "What are the characteristics of Digital Cash?" (PYQ 2025 Grp-A) and "What is e-cash? Explain with example how an online banking system works" (PYQ 2025 Grp-C Q8c)._

**Definition:** E-Cash is a digital representation of physical currency that can be transferred electronically for purchasing goods/services - it is stored as a value on a device (computer, card, digital wallet) rather than as physical notes/coins.

**Characteristics of Digital Cash (E-Cash):**
- **Monetary value** - represents real currency value, convertible
- **Interoperability** - can ideally be used across different systems/platforms
- **Storability and retrievability** - can be stored digitally and retrieved for use
- **Security** - uses cryptographic techniques to prevent forgery and double-spending
- **Anonymity (in some forms)** - certain e-cash systems aim to preserve transaction privacy similar to physical cash

**How online banking enables e-cash style transactions:** A customer's bank account balance is debited electronically and transferred via the bank's secure servers to the merchant's account, with the transaction validated through authentication (OTP, PIN) and recorded instantly in both parties' digital ledgers - functioning as a digital substitute for handing over physical cash.

## **8.5 E-Payment Threats & Protections** ★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: "Discuss the security issues related to E-payments" was a 9-mark question (PYQ 2024 Grp-C Q9a). Pair every threat with its protection mechanism in your answer._

| **Threat** | **Protection Mechanism** |
| --- | --- |
| Card data theft / skimming | Encryption (SSL/TLS), tokenization of card numbers |
| Phishing for payment credentials | User awareness, email/SMS verification, anti-phishing filters |
| Man-in-the-Middle interception | End-to-end encryption, secure protocols (HTTPS, SET) |
| Identity fraud / fake transactions | Two-factor authentication (2FA), OTP verification |
| Replay attacks (resending captured transaction data) | Digital signatures, timestamps, transaction nonces |
| Insider fraud at payment processor | Access control, audit logging, segregation of duties |
| Chargebacks/payment disputes | Clear transaction records, digital receipts, dispute resolution policies |

**Cryptocurrency as an E-Payment mechanism** _(PYQ 2024 Grp-C Q9b - opinion-based question)_: Cryptocurrencies (Bitcoin, Ethereum) offer decentralised, blockchain-secured payments without a central authority - advantages include lower transaction fees for cross-border payments and resistance to fraud via blockchain immutability; concerns include price volatility, regulatory uncertainty, and use in illicit transactions.

# **UNIT 10 - Electronic Data Interchange (EDI)**

_Priority: MUST DO | PYQ Frequency: ★★★★★ every year_

## **10.1 Meaning of EDI** ★★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: EDI is asked almost every year in some form - "What is EDI?", "Discuss EDI architecture", "Identify steps in EDI implementation" all appear across the 3 papers provided. This is one of the highest-yield topics in the entire syllabus._

**Definition:** Electronic Data Interchange (EDI) is the direct, computer-to-computer exchange of standard business documents (purchase orders, invoices, shipping notices, payment confirmations) between trading partners, in a structured electronic format - without manual re-entry or paper documents.

## **10.2 Benefits of EDI** ★★★★

- **Speed** - transactions processed in minutes instead of days (no postal delay)
- **Accuracy** - eliminates manual data entry errors
- **Cost reduction** - saves on paper, printing, postage, and labour
- **Improved cash flow** - faster invoice processing leads to faster payment cycles
- **Better business relationships** - reliable, fast data exchange strengthens trading partnerships
- **Inventory/supply chain efficiency** - real-time data supports accurate inventory and demand planning

## **10.3 Concepts & Basic Components of EDI** ★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: "Briefly describe the basic components of EDI" (PYQ 2025 Grp-C Q11b)._

**Basic Components of EDI:**
- **Standard document format** - structured data standards (e.g., ANSI X12, EDIFACT) that both parties' systems understand
- **Translation software** - converts internal application data into the EDI standard format and vice versa
- **Communication network/method** - the channel used to transmit EDI documents (VAN, internet/AS2, FTP)
- **Trading partner agreement** - a legal/technical agreement defining how the two parties will exchange and process EDI documents

## **10.4 Application of EDI**

EDI is widely applied in:

- **Purchase order processing** - automated PO generation and transmission to suppliers
- **Invoicing** - electronic invoice exchange between buyer and seller
- **Shipping & logistics notifications** - advance shipping notices (ASN) between supply chain partners
- **Inventory management** - automatic stock level updates shared with suppliers (enables Vendor Managed Inventory - VMI)
- **Banking & finance** - electronic funds transfer instructions, payment remittance advice
- **Customs & government filing** - electronic submission of trade/customs documents

## **10.5 EDI Model / Architecture** ★★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: "Discuss EDI architecture" (2023 Grp-B Q6) - draw the flow diagram for high marks._

**EDI Communication Model (flow):**

1. **Originating application** generates business data (e.g., a purchase order in the sender's internal system)
2. **EDI Translator software** converts this internal data into a standard EDI format (e.g., ANSI X12 850 for purchase orders)
3. **Communication/transmission** - the formatted EDI document is sent via a VAN (Value Added Network) or direct internet connection (AS2 protocol) to the trading partner
4. **Receiving EDI Translator** at the partner's end converts the standard EDI format back into data their internal application understands
5. **Receiving application** processes the data automatically (e.g., the order is entered into the supplier's order management system) - no manual re-keying needed

_📝 EXAM TIP: Draw the EDI communication model flow as a box diagram (Sender App → Translator → VAN/Network → Translator → Receiver App). High-scoring diagram!_

**Steps in EDI Implementation** _(PYQ 2024 Grp-B Q3)_:
1. Identify business processes/documents suitable for EDI
2. Select EDI standards (ANSI X12 / EDIFACT) appropriate to the industry/region
3. Choose EDI software/translator and communication method (VAN or AS2/internet)
4. Set up trading partner agreements and test data exchange
5. Pilot test with one trading partner before full rollout
6. Go live and monitor for errors/exceptions

## **10.6 Role of EDI in E-Commerce** ★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: "What role does it [EDI] play in E-Commerce?" (PYQ 2025 Grp-C Q11c)._

EDI forms the backbone of **B2B e-commerce** by enabling automated, paperless transaction processing between businesses - allowing large-scale, high-volume trading relationships (retailer-supplier, manufacturer-distributor) to operate with speed and accuracy that manual processes cannot match. It directly supports Supply Chain Management (Unit 7) by enabling real-time data flow for procurement and inventory decisions.

## **10.7 Protocols: UN/EDIFACT vs ANSI X12** ★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: "Explain EDIFACT" was asked directly (PYQ 2025 Grp-C Q11d). The comparison table below also functions as that answer plus more._

| **Dimension** | **UN/EDIFACT (GTDI)** | **ANSI X12** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Full Form | United Nations Electronic Data Interchange for Administration, Commerce and Transport (Global Trade Data Interchange) | American National Standards Institute - Accredited Standards Committee X12 |
| Developed by | United Nations (international standard) | ANSI (US national standard) |
| Geographic usage | Used predominantly in Europe and internationally for cross-border trade | Used predominantly in the United States and North America |
| Structure | Uses a more flexible, internationally standardised segment/message structure | Uses transaction sets identified by 3-digit numbers (e.g., 850 = Purchase Order) |
| Industry scope | General-purpose, broad international trade applicability | Strong adoption across US retail, healthcare, logistics industries |
| Interoperability | Better suited for international/multi-country trading partners | Better suited for domestic US-based trading relationships |

## **10.8 Data Encryption in EDI: DES vs RSA** ★★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: "Differentiate between public key and private key cryptography" (PYQ 2024 Grp-C Q11a) and "Write the algorithm of RSA" (PYQ 2024 Grp-C Q11c) - RSA algorithm steps are HIGH YIELD, memorise them._

| **Dimension** | **DES (Data Encryption Standard)** | **RSA (Rivest-Shamir-Adleman)** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Type | Symmetric-key encryption | Asymmetric-key (public-key) encryption |
| Key used | Same single key for both encryption and decryption | Two mathematically linked keys - a Public key (encrypt) and a Private key (decrypt) |
| Key length | 56-bit key (considered weak/outdated today) | Typically 1024-4096 bit keys (much stronger) |
| Speed | Fast - suitable for encrypting large volumes of data | Slower - computationally intensive, typically used for smaller data (like encrypting a session key, or for digital signatures) |
| Key distribution problem | Difficult - both parties must securely share the SAME secret key in advance | Easier - the public key can be shared openly; only the private key must be kept secret |
| Typical use case | Bulk data encryption within EDI/VPN tunnels | Digital signatures, secure key exchange, certificate-based authentication |
| Security basis | Substitution-permutation network operating on 64-bit blocks | Based on the mathematical difficulty of factoring large prime numbers |

**RSA Algorithm (basic steps):**
1. Choose two large prime numbers, **p** and **q**
2. Compute **n = p × q** (this becomes part of both public and private keys)
3. Compute Euler's totient: **φ(n) = (p-1)(q-1)**
4. Choose a public exponent **e** such that 1 < e < φ(n) and e is coprime to φ(n)
5. Compute the private exponent **d** such that **(d × e) mod φ(n) = 1**
6. **Public Key = (e, n)**; **Private Key = (d, n)**
7. **Encryption:** Ciphertext **C = M^e mod n** (using receiver's public key)
8. **Decryption:** Plaintext **M = C^d mod n** (using receiver's private key)

_Memory trick: **"Pick Primes, Quotient n, Find Phi, Encrypt-exponent e, Decrypt-exponent d"** for the RSA key generation order._

# **UNIT 11 - Risk of E-Commerce: Security**

_Priority: MUST DO | PYQ Frequency: ★★★★★ every year_

## **11.1 Overview of E-Commerce Risk & Security**

E-Commerce introduces unique risks beyond traditional commerce because transactions, payment data, and personal information all travel over open networks. Effective security requires a layered approach combining technical controls (firewalls, encryption) with procedural controls (key management, certificates) - this is the core theme tying together this entire unit.

**Core security requirements for any e-commerce system:**
- **Confidentiality** - sensitive data is accessible only to authorised parties
- **Integrity** - data is not altered in transit
- **Authentication** - verifying the identity of both parties in a transaction
- **Non-repudiation** - neither party can later deny having participated in the transaction
- **Availability** - systems remain accessible and operational

## **11.2 Security Standards for E-Commerce**

Common security standards/protocols include:

- **SSL/TLS (Secure Sockets Layer / Transport Layer Security)** - encrypts data between browser and server
- **SET (Secure Electronic Transaction)** - secures card payment data (see Unit 8.2)
- **PCI-DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard)** - compliance standard for organisations handling card data
- **HTTPS** - the secure version of HTTP, using SSL/TLS encryption

**Services provided by SSL** _(PYQ 2024 Grp-C Q11b)_:
- **Encryption** - scrambles data so it cannot be read if intercepted
- **Authentication** - verifies the server's (and optionally client's) identity using digital certificates
- **Data integrity** - ensures data isn't tampered with during transmission (via message authentication codes)

## **11.3 Firewall** ★★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: "What is Firewall?" / "Define firewall and state its functions" has appeared in EVERY single paper provided (2023, 2024, 2025). This is close to a guaranteed question - know it cold._

**Definition:** A Firewall is a network security device/software that monitors and controls incoming and outgoing network traffic based on predetermined security rules, acting as a barrier between a trusted internal network and untrusted external networks (like the internet).

**Functions of a Firewall:**
- Filters traffic based on IP address, port number, and protocol
- Blocks unauthorised access attempts from external networks
- Permits legitimate traffic while denying suspicious connections
- Can log traffic for monitoring and auditing purposes
- Some advanced firewalls perform deep packet inspection to detect application-layer threats

**Denial of Service (DoS) attacks** _(PYQ 2025 Grp-C Q10c, asked alongside CPE)_: An attack that floods a target server/network with excessive traffic or requests, overwhelming its capacity and making the service unavailable to legitimate users. A firewall (combined with rate-limiting and traffic filtering) is one defence layer against such attacks, though dedicated DDoS mitigation services are often needed for large-scale attacks.

## **11.4 Cryptography** ★★★★★

**Definition:** Cryptography is the science/practice of securing information by transforming it into an unreadable format (encryption) that can only be reverted to its original readable form (decryption) by someone possessing the correct key.

> See Unit 10.8 for the detailed DES vs RSA comparison and the RSA algorithm - that content applies directly here as the syllabus's "Data Encryption" topic feeds into this unit's "Cryptography" topic.

**Public Key vs Private Key Cryptography:**

| **Dimension** | **Private Key (Symmetric)** | **Public Key (Asymmetric)** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Number of keys | One shared secret key | A key pair - public + private |
| Key secrecy | Both parties must keep the key secret | Public key can be shared freely; only private key is secret |
| Speed | Faster | Slower |
| Example algorithm | DES, AES | RSA |
| Main use | Bulk data encryption | Digital signatures, secure key exchange, authentication |

## **11.5 Key Management**

**Key Management** refers to the processes and systems for generating, distributing, storing, rotating, and revoking cryptographic keys securely throughout their lifecycle.

Key management challenges in e-commerce:
- Securely distributing keys to new trading partners without interception
- Periodically rotating keys to limit damage if a key is compromised
- Revoking compromised keys quickly across all systems
- Safely storing private keys (often using Hardware Security Modules - HSMs)

## **11.6 Password Systems**

Password-based authentication remains a foundational (though weaker) security layer:

- **Single-factor authentication** - password alone (weakest, vulnerable to brute force/phishing)
- **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA/2FA)** - password + OTP/biometric, significantly increases security
- **Password policies** - complexity requirements, expiration, lockout after failed attempts, to reduce brute-force success

## **11.7 Digital Certificates** ★★★★

**Definition:** A Digital Certificate is an electronic credential issued by a trusted Certificate Authority (CA) that binds a public key to the identity of an individual or organisation - verifying that the public key genuinely belongs to the claimed owner.

- Contains: owner's identity, public key, issuing CA's signature, validity period, serial number
- Used to establish trust in SSL/TLS connections (the padlock icon in browsers relies on this)
- **Certificate Revocation List (CRL)** _(PYQ 2024 Grp-A: "Explain what do you mean by certificate revocation list (CRL)")_ - a list maintained by the Certificate Authority of certificates that have been revoked before their expiration date (e.g., due to key compromise) and should no longer be trusted

## **11.8 Digital Signatures** ★★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: "What are digital signatures? How do they differ from digital certificates?" (PYQ 2024 Grp-B Q2) is a classic confusion-pair question - master the distinction below._

**Definition:** A Digital Signature is a cryptographic mechanism that verifies the authenticity and integrity of a digital message or document - created by encrypting a hash of the message with the sender's PRIVATE key. The receiver decrypts it using the sender's PUBLIC key to verify it matches.

**How it works (verification flow):**
1. Sender creates a hash (digest) of the message
2. Sender encrypts the hash with their PRIVATE key → this encrypted hash is the digital signature
3. Sender sends the message + digital signature
4. Receiver decrypts the signature using the sender's PUBLIC key to retrieve the original hash
5. Receiver independently hashes the received message and compares it to the decrypted hash
6. If they match → message is authentic and unaltered

### **11.8.1 Digital Signature vs Digital Certificate** ★★★★★

| **Dimension** | **Digital Signature** | **Digital Certificate** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Purpose | Verifies the authenticity and integrity of a SPECIFIC message/document | Verifies the IDENTITY of a person/organisation by binding them to a public key |
| What it proves | "This specific message was sent by X and wasn't altered" | "This public key genuinely belongs to X" |
| Created by | The sender, using their own private key, for each message | A trusted Certificate Authority (CA), issued to the key owner |
| Analogy | A handwritten signature on a specific letter | An ID card/passport proving who you are |
| Relationship | Uses the key pair that the certificate vouches for | Provides the trusted public key used to verify a digital signature |

# **UNIT 12 - Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP)**

_Priority: MUST DO (highest syllabus weight - 10 lecture hours) | PYQ Frequency: ★★★★★ every year_

## **12.1 What is ERP?** ★★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: "What is ERP? Why is BPR required prior to ERP implementation?" was a direct combined question (PYQ 2025 Grp-C Q9a) - always pair ERP definition with BPR rationale._

**Definition:** Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) is an integrated software system that manages and automates an organisation's core business processes - finance, manufacturing, HR, sales, inventory, supply chain - by consolidating them into a single, unified database and platform, enabling real-time information flow across departments.

## **12.2 Features & Capabilities of ERP**

- **Integration** - unifies data across all departments into one system, eliminating data silos
- **Real-time processing** - business transactions update across all relevant modules instantly
- **Modular architecture** - organisations can implement specific modules (Finance, HR) as needed
- **Standardisation** - enforces consistent business processes across the organisation
- **Scalability** - can grow with the organisation, supporting multi-location/multi-currency operations
- **Reporting & analytics** - centralised data enables comprehensive business intelligence and reporting

## **12.3 Reasons for the Growth of the ERP Market** ★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: "What are the reasons for the growth of ERP market?" (PYQ 2025 Grp-C Q9b)._

- Increasing need for businesses to integrate fragmented legacy systems
- Globalisation requiring standardised processes across multiple countries/currencies
- Growing complexity of supply chains demanding real-time visibility
- Regulatory compliance requirements (financial reporting, tax) needing accurate, centralised data
- Competitive pressure to improve operational efficiency and reduce costs
- Advances in cloud computing making ERP more affordable and accessible (SaaS-based ERP) to smaller businesses

## **12.4 Advantages of ERP System** ★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: "What are the advantages of ERP system?" (PYQ 2025 Grp-C Q9c)._

- Improved efficiency through process automation and reduced manual data entry
- Single source of truth - consistent, accurate data across all departments
- Better decision-making through real-time, integrated reporting
- Enhanced collaboration between departments due to shared data visibility
- Reduced operational costs over time by eliminating redundant systems
- Improved regulatory compliance and audit readiness
- Better customer service through integrated order/inventory/CRM visibility

## **12.5 Re-engineering Work Processes for IT Applications**

Before implementing ERP, organisations typically must re-engineer their existing business processes because legacy manual/ad-hoc processes are often incompatible with the standardised workflows that ERP systems enforce.

## **12.6 Business Process Re-engineering (BPR)** ★★★★★

_📝 EXAM TIP: BPR is the conceptual bridge between Unit 12's "re-engineering work processes" and "Business Process Redesign" mentioned in the syllabus - treat BPR and BPD as essentially the same exam topic with overlapping definitions._

**Definition:** Business Process Re-engineering (BPR) is the radical redesign of core business processes to achieve dramatic improvements in productivity, quality, and efficiency - rather than incrementally improving existing processes, BPR rethinks them from the ground up.

**Why BPR is required prior to ERP implementation:**
- ERP systems impose standardised, best-practice workflows; if old inefficient processes are simply digitised without redesign, the organisation only "automates the mess"
- BPR aligns business processes with what the ERP software is designed to support, maximising the system's benefits
- Without BPR, organisations face costly customisation of the ERP software to fit outdated processes, increasing implementation cost and complexity

### **12.6.1 BPR vs Business Process Redesign (BPD)**

| **Dimension** | **Business Process Re-engineering (BPR)** | **Business Process Redesign (BPD)** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Scope of change | Radical, fundamental rethinking - often starting from a "clean slate" | Often more incremental/moderate restructuring of existing processes |
| Risk level | Higher risk - significant organisational disruption | Lower risk - builds more directly on existing processes |
| Typical trigger | Major competitive threat, need for breakthrough performance gain | Need for moderate efficiency/quality improvement |
| Relationship to ERP | Frequently performed BEFORE/DURING ERP implementation to fit new system | Can be an ongoing, continuous improvement activity, with or without new systems |

## **12.7 Knowledge Engineering and Data Warehouse**

**Knowledge Engineering:** The discipline of building knowledge-based/expert systems that capture and apply organisational expertise to support decision-making - increasingly integrated with ERP analytics for smarter automated decisions.

**Data Warehouse:** A large, centralised repository that stores integrated historical data from multiple ERP modules and other organisational sources, optimised for reporting, analysis, and business intelligence (rather than day-to-day transaction processing, which the operational ERP database handles).

## **12.10 ERP - Present and Future** ★★★★

### **12.10.1 Enterprise Application Integration (EAI)**

**EAI:** A framework/set of technologies that enables different enterprise applications (ERP, CRM, legacy systems) - which may not natively communicate - to share data and processes seamlessly, acting as a middleware layer connecting disparate systems.

### **12.10.2 ERP and E-Commerce**

ERP and E-Commerce integrate to create a seamless order-to-cash process: when a customer places an order on an e-commerce website, the ERP system automatically updates inventory, triggers procurement if needed, processes the financial transaction in accounting, and coordinates with the supply chain/logistics module for fulfilment - eliminating manual handoffs between the customer-facing storefront and back-end operations.

### **12.10.3 ERP and Internet**

The internet enables modern ERP deployment models - particularly **cloud-based/SaaS ERP**, where the system is hosted remotely and accessed via web browsers, removing the need for on-premises infrastructure and enabling real-time access from any location, including mobile and remote work scenarios.

### **12.10.4 Future Directions in ERP** ★★★

- **Cloud ERP / SaaS adoption** - reducing upfront infrastructure costs, enabling faster deployment

## LM-3: One-Line Flashcard Definitions (Group A Speed Round)

> **EOQ:** The order quantity that minimizes total inventory holding and ordering costs.

> **URL:** Uniform Resource Locator - the web address used to locate a resource on the internet.

> **Email Marketing:** Using email to promote products/services and build customer relationships, including cart-recovery and newsletters.

> **VMI (Vendor Managed Inventory):** A supply chain practice where the supplier monitors and manages the buyer's inventory levels, restocking automatically based on agreed parameters.

> **Virtual Storefront:** A website that simulates a physical retail store - displaying products in a catalogue format for online browsing and purchase.

> **E-Procurement:** The business-to-business purchase and sale of supplies/services using the internet.

> **CGI (Common Gateway Interface):** A standard protocol that allows web servers to execute external programs/scripts to generate dynamic web content.

> **Smart/Stored-Value Card:** A card pre-loaded with monetary value that decreases with use; not tied to a bank account (e.g., gift cards, metro cards).

> **Internet Industry Structure:** The layered structure of ISPs (Internet Service Providers), backbone providers, and content/application providers that together deliver internet access and services - often illustrated with ISP-to-backbone-to-content-provider diagrams in answers.
